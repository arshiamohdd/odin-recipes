Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a delicious and flavorful dish made with poached eggs in a rich tomato sauce. It is a popular meal in Middle Eastern and North African cuisine. The sauce is made by cooking tomatoes, onions, garlic, and spices like cumin and paprika. Once the sauce is thick, eggs are gently cracked into it and cooked until the whites are set but the yolks stay runny. People often eat shakshuka with bread to scoop up the sauce and eggs.

Shakshuka is loved because it is easy to make, nutritious, and full of flavor. It can be eaten for breakfast, lunch, or dinner and can be made spicier by adding chili peppers. Some versions include extra ingredients like cheese, bell peppers, or spinach for more variety. This dish is a perfect mix of warm, comforting flavors and is enjoyed by many people around the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 (28-ounce can) whole peeled tomatoes
  • 6 large eggs
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 small bunch fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 small bunch fresh parsley, chopped

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large sauté pan on medium heat. Add the chopped bell pepper and onion and cook for 5 minutes or until the onion becomes translucent.
  2. Add garlic and spices and cook an additional minute.
  3. Pour the can of tomatoes and juice into the pan and break down the tomatoes using a large spoon. Season with salt and pepper and bring the sauce to a simmer.
  4. Use your large spoon to make small wells in the sauce and crack the eggs into each well. Cook the eggs for 5 to 8 minutes, or until the eggs are done to your liking. You can also cover the pan with a lid to expedite the eggs cooking.
  5. Garnish with chopped cilantro and parsley before serving.
